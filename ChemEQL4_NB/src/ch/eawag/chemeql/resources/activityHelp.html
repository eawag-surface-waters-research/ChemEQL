These options correct your log K values (which must be valid for I=0) for the ionic strength.
The activities of all species are divided by the activity-term calculated according to one of the offered approximations, and new log K are calculated accordingly.
Mixed constants are used according to IUPAC convention. Therefore the ion product of water is defined as K={H+}[OH-].
Conditions: - All log K values in the matrix must be valid for I=0.
- All charges of species and components must appear in their names in the matrix as '+' or '-' characters, eg. "HCO3-", "Ca++", "PO4---"
Debye-Hückel-approximation: for I<0.005M
Güntelberg-approximation: for I<0.1M, useful in solutions of several electrolytes.
Davies-approximation: for I<0.5M (const=0.2)
